Here is some important information that will help you do your task properly. Pay close attention to it. 

### How To Navigate the Menu in ServiceNow

* At the top left corner of the screen, find the "Menu" or "All" button.
* Clicking on it will list all modules and applications available to you based on your permissions.
* How to use:
  * **All:** Click "All" or the waffle/grid icon to see all available applications and modules.
  * **Search Bar:** Search for applications, records, or even data directly.
  * **Favorites:** Pin your favorite modules for one-click access.

#### Example: Navigating to the Incident List

1. Go to the Application Navigator (sidebar or menu).
2. Type `Incident` in the search/filter box.
3. Click `Incident > All` to see all incidents.

---

### How to Create a Record in ServiceNow

#### General Steps (Works for Any Table)

1. **Navigate to the Right Module:**

   * Use the **Filter Navigator** (left sidebar or search bar at top).
   * Type the name of the application or table (e.g., `Incident`, `Change`, `User`, `Task`, etc.).
   * Click on the module that says **Create New** or **New** under that application.

     * For example: `Incident > Create New` or `User > New`

2. **Fill Out the Form:**

   * A new form opens for the selected record type.
   * Fill in the required fields (marked with a red asterisk \*), and any other fields you need.

3. **Submit or Save:**

   * Click **Submit** (usually creates the record and returns you to the list view), or
   * Click **Save** (creates the record but keeps you on the form so you can keep editing), or
   * In some interfaces, you may see **Update** (when editing an existing record).


#### Example: Creating a New Incident

1. In the **Filter Navigator**, type `incident`.
2. Click on **Incident > Create New**.
3. The **New Incident** form appears.
4. Enter details, such as:

   * **Caller**
   * **Short Description**
   * **Description**
   * Any other required or relevant fields.
5. Click **Submit** (bottom or top of the form).

### How to Filter Records in a Table in ServiceNow

1. **Navigate to Your Table/List**

   * For example, go to **Incident > All** or any other table you want to view.

2. **Filter by One or Multiple Conditions**

* You'll see a **filter row** above the table (sometimes with "Add Filter" or a funnel icon).
* **Build your filter:**

  1. **Field:** Select the field to filter on (e.g., State, Priority, Assigned To).
  2. **Operator:** Choose the operator (e.g., is, is not, contains, starts with, etc.).
  3. **Value:** Enter or select a value (e.g., "Open" for State).
  4. Click the **AND/OR** button to add more conditions if needed.
* **Run the filter:** Click the **Run** button (funnel icon or "Run") to apply the filter.


#### Example: Filtering Incidents

1. Go to **Incident > All**.
2. Click **Filter** or the funnel icon.
3. Set **State** to **is** **In Progress**.
4. Add **AND Priority is High**.
5. Click **Run**.
6. Only high-priority, in-progress incidents will be displayed.

### How to Impersonate a User in ServiceNow

1. **Click Your User Avatar or Name:**

   * Look for your user name or avatar/photo at the top right corner of the screen.

2. **Select "Impersonate User":**

   * In the dropdown menu, click **Impersonate User**.

3. **Choose the User:**

   * A dialog or search field will appear.
   * Start typing the name or user ID of the person you want to impersonate.
   * Click on their name in the results.

4. **You are now impersonating that user:**

   * You'll see a banner at the top of the screen saying you are impersonating \[user name].
   * The interface changes to reflect the impersonated user's permissions.

5. **To Stop Impersonating:**

   * Click your user name/avatar again.
   * Click **End Impersonation** (or similar option).

### How to Search for a Knowledge Base Article

1. **Locate the Global Search Bar:**

   * The global search bar is usually at the top right of your ServiceNow window.

2. **Enter Your Search Term:**

   * Click in the search bar and type keywords related to your knowledge article (e.g., "VPN setup," "password reset," "onboarding").
   * Hit **Enter** or click the magnifying glass.

3. **Review the Search Results:**

   * The results will display records from **multiple tables** (Incidents, Requests, Knowledge, etc.).
   * Look for a section labeled **Knowledge** or **Knowledge Articles** in the results.

     * In some versions, there's a "Type" or "Table" filter on the side or top—select **Knowledge** if you want only knowledge articles.

4. **Open the Desired Article:**

   * Click the title of the knowledge article you want to view. It opens in a new tab or pane, depending on your UI.

### How to Order a Service Catalog Item in ServiceNow

1. **Go to the Service Catalog**

  * In the left sidebar (Application Navigator), search for and click on **Self-Service > Service Catalog** or simply **Service Catalog**.

2. **Find the Item You Want to Order**

  * Browse: Look through categories (e.g., Hardware, Software, Access, etc.).
  * Search: Scroll the page or use the search bar to enter keywords (e.g., "laptop", "VPN", "Adobe") to find the item you need.
  * Click on the item's name or image to open its details page.

3. **Fill Out the Order Form**

  * Complete any required fields. This can include:

    * Item details (e.g., type, justification, quantity)
    * Delivery information
    * Additional options or comments

   * REMINDER: You can ONLY interact with visible elements. If an element is not both "visible" and "clickable", you cannot interact with it.
      * When selecting a radio or checkbox button, ALWAYS click on the associated label instead of the button.

4. **Submit Your Order**

  * Before submitting, ALWAYS double-check that you have the correct item selected with all the required fields completed.
  * Click the **Order Now**, **Submit**, or **Request** button.
  * You'll receive a confirmation with a request number (e.g., `REQ0001234`).
  * You can track the status of your request from **Self-Service > My Requests** or the "Requests"/"My Items" area in the portal.

### **How to Sort Records in ServiceNow**

1. **Navigate to Your Table/List**

   * For example, go to **Incident > All** or any other table you want to view.

2. **Sort by One or Multiple Columns**

   * `click` on the "show / hide filter" button (funnel icon) at the top left of the page to open the filter row.
   * Repeat the following steps for each column you want to sort by:
     * `click` on the "Add Sort" button to add a new sort filter. This will create a new ordering filter row with two comboboxes under the heading "Order results by the following fields".
     * `fill` the first combobox with the appropriate field name you want to sort by. MAKE SURE to use the exact field name provided.
     * `press` Enter after typing the field name. It is VERY IMPORTANT that you do this before doing anything else. DO NOT click on the run filter button before having confirmed your choice by explicitly pressing ENTER.
     * `select_option` for the appropriate ordering between ascending (a to z) or descending (z to a) in the second combobox.
   * Once all sort filters have been added, `click` the "Run filter" button to apply the sort.

Notes:
   * NEVER directly sort the columns using the table header.
   * NEVER add columns via the Personalize List menu.

3. **Resetting or Clearing Sorting**

   * To reset sorting, click another column, or click again to toggle.
   * In the filter bar, you may see a "Sorted by..." indicator—clear or change it as needed.
